## A. The 1974 Origin Story

1. **June 1974: US–Saudi Joint Commission on Economic Cooperation** (Kissinger). Established military/economic cooperation — F-4/F-5 (later F-15) jets, security guarantees, regime protection. In exchange: implicit understanding Saudi prices oil in USD + recycles surpluses into Treasuries. ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/05/06/nx-s1-5800887/how-the-petrodollar-regime-came-to-be-and-what-losing-it-would-mean-for-the-u-s); [LegalClarity](https://legalclarity.org/the-kissinger-petrodollar-system-history-and-impact/))
2. **Late 1974: Second leg — Treasury Secretary William Simon** negotiated secret arrangements for Saudi to buy Treasuries *off-market* (outside normal auction reporting). ([Mish/CFR commentary](https://mishtalk.com/economics/what-does-cfrs-brad-setser-say-about-petrodollar-myth-and-reality/))
3. **CRITICAL CAVEAT (flag prominently):** There was never a single binding "petrodollar treaty." What expired **June 9, 2024** was the US–Saudi Joint Commission — and the GAO confirms it contained **no formal oil-pricing clause.** The "Saudi ends 50-year petrodollar deal" framing is largely false. ([RFA fact-check](https://www.rfa.org/english/news/afcl/afcl-us-saudi-oil-deal-07082024043619.html); [Birches Group](https://birchesgroup.com/2024/08/29/petrodollar-panic-separating-fact-from-fiction/))
4. The norm was sustained by convention, dollar liquidity, and the riyal-dollar peg — not contract. That's why it erodes at the margin without a single rupture event.

## C. Counter-Arguments (steelman)

**CFR / Setser case.** The petrodollar's geopolitical importance is vastly overstated. Today's global dollar surplus comes from **Asian manufacturing (~$1.5T)**, not Gulf oil (~$200B). Saudi ran CA deficits in 2024–25 — fiscal breakeven > $90/bbl. GCC sovereigns are **net dollar-bond issuers, not net UST buyers**; dollar exposure runs through PIF/ADIA equity stakes. Setser: *"This is fun to believe, but not really based in reality."* US is also a net oil exporter now. ([CFR](https://www.cfr.org/articles/petrodollars-myths-and-reality))

**Basis-trade defense.** Foreign demand didn't fall — it re-domiciled. Cayman + UK + Lux ~$1.7T (Cayman likely undercounted by $1.4T). 8–50× leveraged, dealer balance-sheet–constrained, capable of March-2020-style unwind. Real demand, not sticky.

**Fed-restarts-QE defense.** Possible in principle. But QE during a supply-side oil shock would re-ignite inflation → makes fiscal dominance explicit. Yellen flagged this at AEA Jan 2026.

**De-dollarization is mostly marketing.** USD = 75% SWIFT, CNY = 1.8%. IMF WP/25/178 (Sep 2025): "no robust evidence of effective policy initiatives to reduce dollar reliance" through 2023. Shift is real but slow — ~1.8pp over 2 years.
